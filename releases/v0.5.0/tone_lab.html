Keyboard Shortcuts

Sequencer · FAQ

The basics

The sequencer is a 4 × 16 grid of steps (64 total). The LEN selector controls how many steps actually play; everything past LEN is greyed out and ignored. BPM drives the tempo; one step = one 16th-note.

Each step holds either a rest, a single note, or a chord of up to 4 notes (polyphony). Step 1 of each row is the downbeat; the faint pink hairline every 4 steps marks beat boundaries.

Editing notes on a step

click — bump the note up by one scale tone (sequence is always quantized to the active scale).

right-click — bump down by one semitone (or one scale tone).

drag vertically — pitch shift the whole chord, ±1 semitone per ~10 px of travel. Hold shift while clicking to audition without changing.

right-drag vertically — same gesture but each ~10 px = one octave. Notes that would clip past the keyboard stay at the boundary.

alt+click — replace the step with a rest.

cmd+drag (Ctrl on Windows) — drag a step onto another step to copy everything from source to target: notes, all four dots, and any param-locks.

Polyphony — adding notes to a step

The lime ⊕ at the bottom-right of each step adds a new note (a third above the top of the chord). The ⊖ at the bottom-left removes the top note. Up to 4 voices per step. Chords are played simultaneously and shown stacked in the cell.

The four corner dots — what each one does

RETRIG (top-left, red) — when lit, this step retriggers the synth multiple times within its duration. Use the global RETRIG toggle + HITS + PROB to control. Off-by-default for normal playback.

RAND (top-right, green) — when lit, this step plays a fresh random on-scale note on every play-through, within ±1 octave of its stored note. The cell pulses green when the dice rolls. The stored note isn't mutated; only the live tick.

MIGRATE (bottom-left, purple) — when lit, this step might "migrate" to a randomly chosen row at the same column position. Use the global MIGRATE toggle + PROB slider in the header. The source cell flashes purple when migration fires.

PARAM LOCK (bottom-right, gray square) — clicks into "edit-lock" mode for this step. While in edit mode, any synth or FX slider you tweak is recorded as a per-step override that only applies on this step. Click the square again to exit; the step glows gold while editing.

Scale, root, and ROOT BIAS

The SCALE + ROOT selectors define the active scale (Iwato by default). Every click, drag, randomise, evolve, and migrate operation always snaps to the active scale — there is no off-scale state.

ROOT BIAS shapes the random distribution. Far-left (BIAS 0) locks step 1 to the tonic and weights the rest of the steps heavily toward the tonic. Far-right (BIAS 1, "UNIF") gives every scale tone equal probability on every step.

Migrate, retrig, randomize — global vs per-step

The global toggles in the header (MIGRATE ON, RETRIG ON) apply the effect to all steps. Toggle them OFF and only the per-step dotted cells get the effect. PROB rolls the dice each time the step plays.

This means you can mix-and-match: dot a few steps and leave the global toggles off (= effect fires only on those steps) or dot none and turn the global on (= every step is eligible).

Param-locks (Elektron-style per-step overrides)

Click the gray bottom-right square to enter lock-edit mode for that step. The step glows gold. Now any synth or FX slider you move is recorded as a per-step override — only this step plays with that value. Click the square again to exit edit mode.

Click multiple squares to record the same override across many steps simultaneously. CLEAR ALL in the LOCKS box wipes every lock at once.

Length, transpose, and shortcuts

LEN (1–64) is the step count; everything past LEN is inactive but its values are preserved.

The ±SEMI and ±OCT buttons transpose the entire sequence in place. CLEAR SEQ wipes everything but offers UNDO via the toast for 5 seconds.

Press ? any time for the full keyboard-shortcut palette; space starts/stops playback.

RAND STEPS button vs the green dot — what's the difference?

The RAND STEPS button in the header re-randomises every step's stored value once — destructive, replaces what's there. Useful for "give me a fresh sequence I'll work from."

The green dot on a single step makes that step's note random per play-through — non-destructive, the stored value stays put and a different on-scale pick fires every time.

WAV / MIDI export — what gets exported?

MIDI export writes the sequencer's stored notes to a Standard MIDI File. Per-step locks aren't exported (MIDI has no notion of per-step synth overrides). Chords are exported as simultaneous note-on events.

WAV export renders the full audio chain (synth + FX + master comp) into a 16-bit stereo .wav. You choose the number of repetitions; tail silence is added so reverb / delay can ring out properly.
